Our Story

Built in Oakland.
Rooted in love.

Charity Driver didn't come from a boardroom. It came from someone who grew up watching their community struggle — and decided to do something about it.

Paris Irving

Founder · Innovation Educator · Oakland Native

Paris Irving grew up in East Oakland — a city full of brilliance and resilience, but also real, visible need. As an alumnus and educator at The Hidden Genius Project, Paris has spent years teaching Black male youth that technology can be a tool for change. Charity Driver is that belief in action: using technology not to impress, but to serve.

"I didn't build this because I had to. I built it because I kept driving past the same people every day and thinking — someone should do something. Then I realized that someone was me."
— Paris Irving, Founder
Why Charity Driver exists

Oakland has one of the highest rates of homelessness in the country. But beyond the statistics are real people — with names, stories, and daily needs that systems alone can't meet. Charity Driver was built on a simple belief: that ordinary people, choosing to show up with a meal or a blanket, can do what institutions can't. They can make someone feel seen.

This platform is not about charity as obligation. It's about love as action. The kind that gives without keeping score. The kind that shows up even when it costs something.
